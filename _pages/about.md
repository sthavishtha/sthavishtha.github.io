I am a PhD candidate in the [School of Mechanical Engineering](https://engineering.purdue.edu/ME) at [Purdue University](https://www.purdue.edu), advised by [Prof. Hector Gomez](https://engineering.purdue.edu/ME/People/ptProfile?resource_id=126734). My research focuses on **modeling and simulating interface problems**, with particular interest in multiphase and multi-component flows, fluid–structure interactions, porous media, and reacting flows.

My current work develops high-fidelity computational models for **elasto-capillary fluid–structure interaction** — studying how nonlinear elastic solids interact with multiple immiscible fluids at deformable interfaces. A recent result from this work is *fibrotaxis*: a mechanism enabling gradient-free, spontaneous and controllable droplet transport on soft solids, with direct applications to microfluidics, lab-on-chip devices, and self-cleaning surfaces.

Previously, I worked on turbulent reacting flows at the [Institute of Fluid Dynamics, ETH Zurich](https://www.ifd.mavt.ethz.ch/), and on lattice Boltzmann modeling for conjugate heat transfer and rarefied gas flows. I completed my undergraduate education at NIT Karnataka, India, where I first explored cavity-flow simulations using lattice Boltzmann methods.
